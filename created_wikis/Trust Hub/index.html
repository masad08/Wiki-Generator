 What is TRU?
The Trust Research Unit (TRU) is an initiative by the Corporate Patient Safety & Quality at Dr. Sulaiman Alhabib Medical Group (HMG) to establish a dedicated research unit focused on healthcare quality and patient safety.

Why was TRU created?
  • To position HMG as a leader in patient safety and quality research.

  • To generate evidence that improves clinical and operational practices.

  • To build research capacity among staff and foster collaboration across hospitals and medical centers.

  • To contribute to high-impact publications and present findings nationally and internationally


Who Does TRU Serve?
TRU supports all hospitals and Habib Medical Centers (HMCs) within HMG by:
  • Providing research infrastructure and guidance.
  • Supporting staff in conducting research projects.
  • Helping analyze and publish research from HMG’s data.
  • Creating opportunities for professional growth in research.

IHI Open School at Dr. Sulaiman Alhabib Medical Group
As part of our ongoing commitment to improving patient safety and healthcare quality, Dr. Sulaiman Alhabib Medical Group has partnered with the Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) to provide access to the IHI Open School. This initiative, led at the corporate level, offers a structured learning pathway for staff to develop their expertise in quality improvement, patient safety, leadership, and system design.


What is IHI Open School?

IHI Open School is an internationally recognized learning platform designed to equip healthcare professionals with the knowledge and skills necessary to drive meaningful improvements in healthcare systems. Through a series of interactive courses, participants gain insights into essential concepts such as reducing medical errors, improving patient care processes, and implementing evidence-based safety strategies.

This initiative is designed for all HMG staff who are eager to enhance their understanding of quality improvement and patient safety. Whether you are a clinician, a nurse, a hospital administrator, or a member of the quality and patient safety teams, these courses provide valuable, practical knowledge that can be applied directly to your daily work.


How to Participate

Seats for IHI Open School are limited and are offered on a registration and nomination basis. If you are interested in securing a seat and gaining access to these courses, please submit your request by filling out the interest form [link]. Once approved, you will receive further details on how to access the courses and begin your learning journey.


What Courses Are Available?
Participants will have access to a wide range of topics relevant to their roles, including:
  • Quality Improvement (QI)
    • Fundamentals of QI methodologies
    • Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA) cycles
    • Using data for improvement
  • Patient Safety
    • Identifying and preventing medical errors
    • Root cause analysis and failure modes
    • Safe medication practices
  • Leadership & Change Management
    • Building a culture of safety
    • Leading quality improvement initiatives
    • Effective teamwork and communication in healthcare
  • Health System Improvement
    • Designing safer and more efficient healthcare processes
    • Reducing variability in care
    • Enhancing patient-centered care

Trigger Tools - summary

What Are Trigger Tools?
Trigger Tools are structured methodologies used to detect potential adverse events in healthcare settings by identifying specific "triggers" that indicate possible harm or errors in patient care. These triggers act as warning signs that prompt further investigation, helping healthcare providers identify, analyze, and prevent patient safety incidents.
Developed by the Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) and other global patient safety organizations, Trigger Tools are widely used in hospitals and healthcare systems worldwide to enhance patient safety and reduce harm. Unlike traditional incident reporting systems, which rely on voluntary reporting, Trigger Tools proactively search for potential adverse events within medical records and clinical data.

How Do Trigger Tools Work?
A trigger is a clue within a patient’s medical record that suggests the possibility of an adverse event.
Common triggers include:
  • Abnormal lab results (e.g., high creatinine levels may indicate kidney injury).
  • Use of antidotes (e.g., administration of naloxone may indicate an opioid overdose).
  • Unplanned readmissions (e.g., a patient returning to the hospital within 30 days may signal a complication).

However, this is based on the hospital settings. What applies to a hospital may not apply to others. Same for private and governmental hospitals.
Once identified, trained reviewers analyze the cases to determine whether an actual adverse event occurred and how it could have been prevented.

References:
Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI). "IHI Global Trigger Tool for Measuring Adverse Events." Available at: www.ihi.org
Classen, D.C., et al. (2011). "Global Trigger Tool Shows That Adverse Events in Hospitals May Be Ten Times Greater Than Previously Measured." Health Affairs, 30(4).

Trigger Tools Initiative at HMG
Corporate Patient Safety & Quality is implementing Trigger Tools as a corporate initiative to strengthen patient safety and improve healthcare quality across hospitals and medical centers.


How We Are Implementing Trigger Tools at HMG
The initiative follows a phased approach to ensure feasibility, effectiveness, and sustainability (see illustration below):

1. Selection of Trigger Tools
A review of internationally validated Trigger Tools is being conducted to determine the most relevant ones for HMG hospitals and medical centers.
The selected tools will align with HMG’s patient safety priorities, focusing on areas such as medication safety, surgical safety, and ICU care.
2. Hospital Engagement and Feasibility Testing
Initially, a group of selected hospitals will participate in feasibility assessments.
Discussions will be held with subject matter experts (physicians, pharmacists, nurses, and quality teams) to evaluate the applicability of the selected Trigger Tools.
3. Pre-Implementation Audit
A baseline audit will be conducted to assess the current state of patient safety incidents and adverse event detection in participating hospitals.
The audit will help determine how well the selected Trigger Tools integrate into existing workflows and identify potential challenges before full-scale implementation.
4. Pilot Implementation
If the feasibility assessments and audits confirm readiness, the Trigger Tools will be implemented in selected hospitals.
Training sessions will be provided to ensure that clinical and quality teams are equipped to use the tools effectively.
5. Evaluation and Expansion
The initial implementation will be monitored closely, with regular evaluations to measure effectiveness.
Based on findings, the initiative will be expanded to more HMG hospitals and medical centers, ensuring a standardized approach to adverse event detection.



Why This Matters to You
  • For Healthcare Providers: Helps detect hidden patient safety risks and enables proactive intervention to prevent harm.
  • For Quality and Safety Teams: Provides a structured, data-driven approach to monitoring and improving patient outcomes.
  • For Hospital Administrators: Supports system-wide safety improvements and ensures compliance with international best practices.
