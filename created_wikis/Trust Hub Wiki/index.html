What is TRU?
The Trust Research Unit (TRU) is an initiative by the Corporate Patient Safety & Quality at Dr. Sulaiman Al-Habib Medical Group to establish a dedicated research unit focused on healthcare quality and patient safety.

Why was TRU created?
To position HMG as a leader in patient safety and quality research.
To generate evidence that improves clinical and operational practices.
To build research capacity among staff and foster collaboration across hospitals and medical centers.
To contribute to high-impact publications and present findings nationally and internationally.


Who Does TRU Serve?
TRU supports all hospitals and Habib Medical Centers (HMCs) within HMG by:
  • Providing research infrastructure and guidance.
  • Supporting staff in conducting research projects.
  • Helping analyze and publish research from HMG’s data.
  • Creating opportunities for professional growth in research.

Trigger Tools

Trigger Tools - summary


What Are Trigger Tools?
Trigger Tools are structured methodologies used to detect potential adverse events in healthcare settings by identifying specific "triggers" that indicate possible harm or errors in patient care. These triggers act as warning signs that prompt further investigation, helping healthcare providers identify, analyze, and prevent patient safety incidents.
Developed by the Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) and other global patient safety organizations, Trigger Tools are widely used in hospitals and healthcare systems worldwide to enhance patient safety and reduce harm. Unlike traditional incident reporting systems, which rely on voluntary reporting, Trigger Tools proactively search for potential adverse events within medical records and clinical data.


How Do Trigger Tools Work?
A trigger is a clue within a patient’s medical record that suggests the possibility of an adverse event.
Common triggers include:
Abnormal lab results (e.g., high creatinine levels may indicate kidney injury).
Use of antidotes (e.g., administration of naloxone may indicate an opioid overdose).
Unplanned readmissions (e.g., a patient returning to the hospital within 30 days may signal a complication).

However, this is based on the hospital settings. What applies to a hospital may not apply to others. Same for private and governmental hospitals.
Once identified, trained reviewers analyze the cases to determine whether an actual adverse event occurred and how it could have been prevented.

Trigger Tools Initiative at HMG
Corporate Patient Safety & Quality is implementing Trigger Tools as a corporate initiative to strengthen patient safety and improve healthcare quality across hospitals and medical centers.


How We Are Implementing Trigger Tools at HMG
The initiative follows a phased approach to ensure feasibility, effectiveness, and sustainability (see illustration below):
1. Selection of Trigger Tools
A review of internationally validated Trigger Tools is being conducted to determine the most relevant ones for HMG hospitals and medical centers.
The selected tools will align with HMG’s patient safety priorities, focusing on areas such as medication safety, surgical safety, and ICU care.
2. Hospital Engagement and Feasibility Testing
Initially, a group of selected hospitals will participate in feasibility assessments.
Discussions will be held with subject matter experts (physicians, pharmacists, nurses, and quality teams) to evaluate the applicability of the selected Trigger Tools.
3. Pre-Implementation Audit
A baseline audit will be conducted to assess the current state of patient safety incidents and adverse event detection in participating hospitals.
The audit will help determine how well the selected Trigger Tools integrate into existing workflows and identify potential challenges before full-scale implementation.
4. Pilot Implementation
If the feasibility assessments and audits confirm readiness, the Trigger Tools will be implemented in selected hospitals.
Training sessions will be provided to ensure that clinical and quality teams are equipped to use the tools effectively.
5. Evaluation and Expansion
The initial implementation will be monitored closely, with regular evaluations to measure effectiveness.
Based on findings, the initiative will be expanded to more HMG hospitals and medical centers, ensuring a standardized approach to adverse event detection.


Why This Matters to You
  • For Healthcare Providers: Helps detect hidden patient safety risks and enables proactive intervention to prevent harm.
  • For Quality and Safety Teams: Provides a structured, data-driven approach to monitoring and improving patient outcomes.
  • For Hospital Administrators: Supports system-wide safety improvements and ensures compliance with international best practices.

IHI Open School at Dr. Sulaiman Al-Habib Medical Group

What is IHI Open School?
IHI Open School is an internationally recognized learning platform designed to equip healthcare professionals with the knowledge and skills necessary to drive meaningful improvements in healthcare systems. Through a series of interactive courses, participants gain insights into essential concepts such as reducing medical errors, improving patient care processes, and implementing evidence-based safety strategies.

This initiative is designed for all HMG staff who are eager to enhance their understanding of quality improvement and patient safety. Whether you are a clinician, a nurse, a hospital administrator, or a member of the quality and patient safety teams, these courses provide valuable, practical knowledge that can be applied directly to your daily work.


How to Participate
Seats for IHI Open School are limited and are offered on a registration and nomination basis. HMG QIP Award

Created: 23/03/2025 | Last modified: 24/03/2025 | Author: Corporate Patient Safety & Quality
The Quality Improvement Project (QIP) Award is a strategic initiative developed by the Corporate Patient Safety & Quality Department at Dr. Sulaiman Al-Habib Medical Group (HMG). The award seeks to identify, encourage, and reward practical healthcare improvement projects led by hospital staff members. The fundamental purpose is to actively engage frontline healthcare professionals, support staff, and administrative teams in identifying everyday clinical or operational challenges, proposing realistic solutions, and successfully implementing them to achieve tangible, measurable improvements.

Participation in the award is open to all employees within HMG, reflecting the organization's belief that meaningful quality improvement ideas can originate from any level or role within the group. Teams are particularly encouraged to apply, recognizing that effective quality improvements often result from diverse collaborations and combined expertise.

Projects submitted for consideration must directly contribute to enhancing patient care, hospital efficiency, or overall service quality. To ensure relevance, the initiative emphasizes that submissions must be original and reflect recent efforts—projects implemented within the preceding year. Moreover, all proposed improvements must strictly comply with the group’s existing policies, ethical guidelines, and safety standards.

Participants follow a structured submission approach, starting with the initial declaration of project intent submitted to the Corporate Quality Department. Following this step, dedicated support, training, and guidance are provided to teams by their local quality departments, helping refine their proposals, select appropriate quality improvement methodologies, and develop robust implementation plans.

The final submission is expected to clearly outline the nature and scope of the project, detail the practical methods used, describe the resources involved, and highlight any challenges encountered. Crucially, submissions must include evidence of their impact, such as data showing improved patient outcomes or enhanced operational efficiency. Teams are also expected to share reflections and insights gained during the project's implementation, emphasizing any lessons learned and suggestions for future improvement efforts.

Each submission undergoes careful review based on specific evaluation criteria, including the project’s relevance to healthcare quality, uniqueness of approach, effectiveness of execution, clear evidence of success, and potential for sustainability and scalability across the hospital group. Additionally, the alignment of the initiative with HMG's strategic goals and values is considered essential.

The award serves not only as recognition of significant professional achievements but also aims to disseminate successful methods across the wider hospital group. Winning initiatives receive acknowledgment, public recognition within HMG, and financial incentives totaling  up to SAR 250,000 to further encourage quality improvements.

Through the QIP Award, Dr. Sulaiman Al-Habib Medical Group demonstrates its commitment to quality enhancement, supporting meaningful, practical solutions that directly benefit patients, staff, and overall hospital performance. This initiative is a clear reflection of the group's dedication to continuous learning, collaborative improvement, and ongoing excellence in healthcare.

2024/2025 QIP Award

Created: 23/03/2025 | Last modified: 24/03/2025 | Author: Corporate Patient Safety & Quality
The initial cycle of the QIP Award at Dr. Sulaiman Al-Habib Medical Group concluded in a celebratory ceremony held on February 17, 2025. This event recognized and honored the exceptional contributions made by healthcare teams across the institution. Numerous innovative projects were celebrated, highlighting significant efforts to improve patient safety, clinical effectiveness, and overall healthcare quality. While all finalist projects received acknowledgment for their commitment and creativity, five initiatives stood out for their measurable impact and significant contribution to the group's strategic quality goals.



First Place (Tie)
PHLEBOTOMY RE-ASSESSMENT PROGRAM (MD Lab)
Focusing on laboratory practices, this project implemented extensive training, refined workflow protocols, and robust quality control measures, markedly enhancing diagnostic accuracy and patient safety.
NABD Program Improved Door to Balloon Time in STEMI (Rayyan)
This project significantly reduced delays in the emergency management of acute myocardial infarction (STEMI), ensuring timely cardiac intervention and aligning care practices with international standards for emergency cardiovascular care.
Third Place
PRESCRIPSURE: Enhancing Patient Safety through Reducing Prescribing Medication Errors (Takhassusi)
By integrating targeted staff education and advanced healthcare information system optimizations (VIDA), this initiative achieved a significant reduction in prescribing errors, strengthening patient safety and medication management processes​.
Fourth Place
FusionCare: Aligning Quality of Recovery and Anesthesia for Optimal C-Section Patient Satisfaction and Safety (Khobar)
This initiative successfully applied human-factor engineering alongside clinical innovations in anesthetic and surgical care, resulting in enhanced patient satisfaction and fewer postoperative complications following cesarean sections.
Fifth Place
Safe Maternity and Newborn Care: Reduce Complications Associated with Instrumental Delivery (Takhassusi)
Addressing elevated rates of neonatal complications during instrumental deliveries, this initiative introduced comprehensive multidisciplinary training and standardized protocols, substantially decreasing neonatal complications from 6% to just 1.44%, surpassing established benchmarks​.


The ceremony also provided a platform to recognize and celebrate all finalist projects, each reflecting the dedication, ingenuity, and teamwork essential to continuous improvement in healthcare. Collectively, the finalists and winners showcased the group's ongoing commitment to patient-centered care and clinical excellence.
